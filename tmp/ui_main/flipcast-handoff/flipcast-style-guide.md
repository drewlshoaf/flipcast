# Flipcast Style Guide

## Brand personality

Flipcast should feel:

- playful
- bright
- creative
- immediate
- modern
- consumer-friendly
- confident without feeling corporate

The visual language should suggest that powerful generation technology is underneath the experience, but the product should still feel light, intuitive, and fun.

---

## Visual system

## Backgrounds
Use light, airy backgrounds with subtle multicolor atmosphere.

Preferred treatment:
- pale sky / white base
- soft radial gradients
- faint transitions through blue, pink, mint, and lavender-adjacent tones

Example direction:
- base white / pale blue
- sky glow in one corner
- pink glow in another
- green glow in another

## Surfaces
Use:
- white cards
- semi-translucent white cards
- glassy or frosted feeling surfaces
- light borders
- soft blur where useful

Avoid:
- overly flat sterile white with no depth
- dark heavy panels
- grayscale-only controls

## Corners
Use generous radii throughout:
- pills: fully rounded
- cards: rounded-2xl to rounded-[30px] range
- larger hero / feature surfaces: rounded-[34px] to rounded-[40px]

## Shadows
Shadows should be:
- soft
- diffused
- slightly tinted by accent colors
- non-enterprise / non-harsh

Use blue, pink, and green-tinted shadow environments sparingly.

---

## Color direction

## Primary palette
- Sky blue
- Pink
- Mint / emerald green

## Supporting palette
- Very light sky backgrounds
- Very light pink backgrounds
- Very light green backgrounds
- Soft slate for text
- White for surfaces

## Usage
- blue for structure / clarity / primary cool energy
- pink for playfulness / warmth / attention
- green for freshness / success / completion
- combine the three in gradients for CTA and hero emphasis

---

## Typography

## Overall feeling
Typography should feel:
- clean
- modern
- legible
- confident
- friendly

## Headings
Use:
- large, bold, tight-tracking headlines
- simple sentence structure
- emotionally clear statements

Examples:
- Make the show first.
- Go from idea to episode.
- Your player is also your editing surface.

## Body copy
Use:
- short paragraphs
- strong readability
- medium contrast slate text on light surfaces
- plainspoken consumer language

---

## Component styling

## Buttons
Primary buttons:
- multicolor gradient or strong dark contrast depending on context
- rounded-full
- medium-large padding
- bold label
- strong hover clarity

Secondary buttons:
- white or lightly tinted background
- subtle border
- soft shadow
- no heavy chrome

## Chips
Chips should:
- be colorful
- feel tappable
- be lightweight and playful
- use tinted backgrounds by family
  - blue chip
  - pink chip
  - green chip

## Selectable cards
Format and vibe cards should:
- be clearly tappable
- use distinct selected states
- feel like choices, not tiny controls
- maintain strong label hierarchy

Selected state options:
- stronger border
- cleaner white surface
- more defined shadow
- optional gradient accent marker

## Player card
The player should:
- feel friendly and prominent
- not look like a generic audio plugin
- use a bright gradient play button
- present progress clearly
- show key metadata in compact colorful tiles

## Outline / structure cards
These should:
- explain the episode flow
- use pastel-tinted surfaces
- feel like helpful previews rather than dense text blocks

## Idea rail
The idea rail should:
- feel like a discovery panel
- use grouped sections
- use colorful card stacks
- encourage fast-start behavior

---

## UX tone

The UI should constantly imply:

- you can start quickly
- you do not need to get everything perfect
- you can iterate after generation
- making a Flipcast is supposed to be fun

Avoid making the experience feel:
- bureaucratic
- technical for its own sake
- enterprise
- overloaded with controls

---

## Experience principle

**Flipcast is not a settings page. It is a media creation experience.**

That principle should govern layout, hierarchy, color, interaction, and copy choices everywhere.
