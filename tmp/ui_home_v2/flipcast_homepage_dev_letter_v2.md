# Letter to the Development Team

Team,

We have now converged on the right home page direction for Flipcast, and this version should be treated as the reference direction for implementation.

The key idea is that the home page is **not** a studio, editor, dashboard, or settings surface. It is a **consumer media discovery experience** built around a single strong center-stage invitation to start. The page should feel playful, bright, modern, premium, and easy. The design needs to communicate that Flipcast can generate something powerful, but the interface itself should feel light and welcoming rather than technical.

## Core principle

**Flipcast is not a settings page. It is a media creation experience.**

Everything on the page should reinforce that principle.

## What this home page is trying to achieve

When a user lands here, the page should immediately communicate four things:

1. There are many interesting things to start from.
2. Starting is simple and immediate.
3. The product feels alive, cultural, and current.
4. The center of the experience is a single, obvious action.

That means the layout must be organized around a **calm central hero** and a **structured outer orbit of topics**. The earlier versions failed when they tried to scatter too many topics freely around the center. The current direction works better because the center is protected and the topic field is arranged more intentionally.

## Most important implementation rules

### 1. Protect the center lane
The play button, headline, body copy, and CTA row need a quiet vertical lane with breathing room. Nothing should visually fight that area. Topic pills can come near the center, but only in a controlled way and only as short labels near the outside edge of the central hero zone.

### 2. The topic cloud should feel designed, not random
We still want abundance, but not chaos. Topics should live in clear placement groups:
- a top arc
- a left rail
- a right rail
- a few short near-center pills in the northwest and northeast quadrants
- a bottom cluster under the hero

This is important. The abundance should be implied through structured density, not random scattering.

### 3. The top banner matters
The top of the page is now more than simple nav. It includes:
- the Flipcast logo on the left
- an ad promo code form across the upper middle
- a language selector on the right
- login and sign up buttons on the right

That upper strip needs to feel premium, integrated, and lightweight. It should not feel like a utility toolbar from enterprise software.

### 4. The play button is the visual anchor
The play button must feel polished, glossy, dimensional, and premium. It cannot be a flat pink circle. It needs a richer multistep gradient, a luminous highlight, and a subtle multicolor outer glow that ties into the Flipcast palette.

## Tone and visual character

The page should feel:
- playful
- bright
- creative
- immediate
- modern
- consumer-friendly
- confident without feeling corporate

Avoid anything that feels:
- enterprise
- gray and functional
- overly technical
- dashboard-like
- cluttered in the middle

## Final note

Please treat the current concept as a composition problem, not just a component problem. The success of this page depends heavily on spacing, density, and balance. Small misplacements can make the whole page feel off.

The center stage should feel intentional.
The outer topics should feel balanced.
The top banner should feel integrated.
And the play button should feel special.

Build toward that level of polish.
