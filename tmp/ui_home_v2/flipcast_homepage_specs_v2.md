# Flipcast Home Page Specifications

## 1. Overview

This document defines the intended design and layout for the Flipcast home page.

This page is a **discovery-first home page** with a **center-stage hero**. It is not a studio/editor page. It is not a dashboard. It is not a settings page.

The page should present:
- a light, atmospheric pastel background
- a strong center-stage hero
- a structured field of topic pills around the hero
- a top banner containing promo code and account controls
- a premium gradient play button as the visual anchor

---

## 2. Brand and Style Direction

### 2.1 Brand personality
The page must feel:
- playful
- bright
- creative
- immediate
- modern
- consumer-friendly
- confident without feeling corporate

### 2.2 Background treatment
Use:
- a pale, airy base
- soft radial atmospheric gradients
- subtle blue, pink, and mint energy
- very light, premium visual atmosphere

Preferred direction:
- pale sky / pale white base
- blue glow in one area
- pink glow in another
- mint glow in another
- no hard section breaks

Avoid:
- dark backgrounds
- flat sterile white
- harsh color blocking
- anything that feels like enterprise SaaS chrome

### 2.3 Surface styling
Use:
- white or semi-translucent white surfaces
- soft glass / frosted treatments where appropriate
- soft borders
- subtle blur
- diffused shadows

Avoid:
- thick dark borders
- hard black outlines
- flat utility-box styling
- gray enterprise panels

### 2.4 Typography
Headlines:
- large
- bold
- tightly tracked
- short
- emotionally clear

Body copy:
- plainspoken
- short paragraph
- high readability
- medium-contrast slate text

### 2.5 Color palette
Primary direction:
- sky blue
- pink
- mint green

Supporting tones:
- soft lavender
- soft warm yellow
- white
- slate text

Use cases:
- blue = clarity / cool energy
- pink = warmth / playfulness / focus
- green = freshness / liveliness
- purple and yellow = secondary accent variety for topic chips

---

## 3. Overall Layout Structure

The page should be organized into the following zones:

1. Brand/logo zone at top left
2. Top-center promo code banner
3. Top-right utility controls
4. Top arc of topics
5. Left topic rail
6. Right topic rail
7. Small near-center pills in upper-left and upper-right inner quadrants
8. Center-stage hero
9. Bottom topic cluster below the hero

The center must remain the primary focal area.

---

## 4. Top Header / Banner

### 4.1 Left side: brand
Place the Flipcast brand at the top left.

Include:
- small gradient play-style brand mark
- wordmark “flipcast”

The logo should feel playful and polished, not corporate.

### 4.2 Center: ad promo code form
Place a pill-shaped promo code banner across the top center.

Structure:
- pink-tinted label pill on the left, reading something like “Ad promo code”
- rounded input field in the middle
- rounded action button on the right, such as “Apply”

Styling requirements:
- the entire banner should be pill-shaped or very rounded
- white / translucent white surface
- soft shadow
- subtle border
- integrated, premium feel
- should visually read as one banner, not three separate utility controls

Behavioral intent:
- this should feel like an inviting consumer control
- not like an admin form
- not like a checkout coupon input

### 4.3 Right side: utility controls
Place the following controls at the top right:
- language selector
- log in button
- sign up button

Language selector:
- should allow at least English and Spanish
- use a rounded pill treatment
- should visually match the rest of the header system

Log in button:
- white or lightly tinted background
- soft border
- understated

Sign up button:
- stronger contrast
- dark navy fill is acceptable here
- should feel primary among the account controls

Important:
- the old “Studio” button should not appear on this page

---

## 5. Center-Stage Hero

### 5.1 Protected center lane
The center hero needs a clear vertical protected lane.

This area includes:
- play button
- headline
- body copy
- primary and secondary CTA buttons

Rules:
- no long topic pills should intrude into this lane
- only a few short near-center pills may approach it
- the hero needs visible breathing room above, left, and right

### 5.2 Hero glow
Behind the center hero, use a soft white atmospheric glow.

Purpose:
- helps separate the hero from surrounding topic content
- keeps the center readable
- makes the hero feel intentional rather than floating

Requirements:
- soft-edged
- diffused
- elliptical / radial
- should not look like a card panel
- should feel atmospheric, not literal

---

## 6. Play Button

### 6.1 Importance
The play button is the main visual anchor of the page.

It must:
- feel premium
- feel glossy
- feel dimensional
- feel more like a polished app icon / hero object than a flat button

### 6.2 Shape and size
Use:
- large circular form
- centered in the hero
- large enough to dominate the first view

### 6.3 Gradient requirements
The play button gradient is important.

It should include:
- a rich pink / magenta core
- transitions into warmer pink
- transitions into purple
- a subtle mint / teal edge influence

The button should not be a simple two-color gradient.

Preferred gradient behavior:
- brighter pink highlight near upper-left
- deeper pink / magenta in the main core
- purple and cool tone blending toward the lower/right edge
- subtle multicolor perimeter glow that harmonizes with the overall Flipcast palette

### 6.4 Finish
Include:
- glossy highlight
- soft inner sheen
- subtle outer ring / aura
- white play triangle centered inside

Avoid:
- flat pink circle
- harsh neon effect
- overdone skeuomorphism
- muddy color transitions

---

## 7. Headline and Copy

### 7.1 Headline
Use:
- “You make the show.”

Styling:
- “You” should use a blue-pink-green gradient text treatment
- the rest of the headline should be dark text
- large, bold, tight tracking
- centered beneath the play button

### 7.2 Supporting copy
Use a short paragraph under the headline.

The copy should explain that users can drop in a topic and immediately get a playable Flipcast episode.

Styling:
- centered
- moderate width
- slate text
- high readability
- not too wide

### 7.3 CTA row
Directly beneath the supporting copy, include:
- primary CTA button such as “Start a flip”
- secondary CTA such as “Surprise me”

Primary CTA:
- gradient treatment tied to the palette
- rounded full
- clearly primary

Secondary CTA:
- white or lightly tinted
- subtle border
- lower emphasis than the primary

---

## 8. Topic Placement System

This is one of the most important parts of the page.

The topics should feel abundant, but the abundance must be structured.

### 8.1 Topic groups
Topics should be distributed in five main placement groups:

#### A. Top arc
A row / arc of small topic pills above the hero.

Requirements:
- should span the upper middle width
- should feel like a gentle arc or band
- should not sit too close to the play button
- should not overlap one another

Use shorter pill lengths here.

#### B. Left rail
A vertical rail of topic pills on the left side.

Requirements:
- start below the top arc
- stack downward with consistent spacing
- vary widths and colors
- use a mix of short and medium-length phrases
- should feel balanced against the right rail

#### C. Right rail
A vertical rail of topic pills on the right side.

Requirements:
- mirror the visual weight of the left rail
- should not feel sparser or heavier than the left
- use mixed pill lengths and mixed colors
- maintain clear gaps between items

#### D. Near-center quadrant pills
Add a few short pills in the northwest and northeast quadrants near the outer edge of the hero zone.

Examples of acceptable short lengths:
- lore again?
- creator economy?
- nostalgia wave?
- trend fatigue?
- soft launch?

Rules:
- these should be short
- these should not overlap the hero
- these should feel like satellites near the center
- they should sit outside the main hero lane, not inside it

#### E. Bottom cluster
Place a centered cluster of short pills below the main CTA area.

Requirements:
- should be positioned clearly below the CTA row
- should be centered overall
- should use wrapped layout or structured positioning with no overlap
- should feel like a lighter continuation of the topic field, not a second hero

### 8.2 Topic density
The page should feel “content-rich,” but not chaotic.

Guidelines:
- more density at the edges and rails
- lighter density near the hero
- balanced left/right
- a few pills above and below the hero for continuity
- no topic overlap
- no random bunching

### 8.3 Pill styling
Topic pills should:
- be rounded / capsule-like
- have tinted pastel backgrounds
- have light borders
- feel lightweight and tappable
- use color families like blue, pink, green, purple, yellow

Do not make them look like large cards or heavy blocks.

### 8.4 Topic lengths
Use a mix of:
- medium descriptive phrases on outer rails
- shorter, punchier phrases near the center
- short compact phrases in the bottom cluster

This creates hierarchy.

---

## 9. Spacing and Balance Rules

### 9.1 Top spacing
Everything should sit comfortably down from the very top edge.
The design should not feel cramped against the browser top.

### 9.2 Center breathing room
The hero needs visible separation:
- from the top arc above
- from the near-center pills
- from the bottom cluster below

### 9.3 Left/right balance
The visual mass of the topics should feel intentionally balanced.
It does not have to be perfectly symmetrical, but it should feel composed.

### 9.4 Overlap rules
No topic pills should overlap:
- each other
- the play button
- the headline
- the body copy
- the CTA row

Near-center pills may visually approach the hero boundary, but they should not physically collide with it.

---

## 10. Interaction and Motion Guidance

If hover states are implemented:
- slight lift
- slight brightness increase
- slightly stronger shadow

Do not use:
- aggressive scaling
- harsh glow pulses
- enterprise-like hover outlines

The page should feel smooth and lively, not overly animated.

---

## 11. Responsive Behavior

When adapting to smaller screens:
- preserve the center-stage hierarchy
- simplify topic density
- keep the hero readable
- maintain spacing around the center
- allow topic rails to collapse or reduce as needed
- keep the top promo banner usable and visually coherent
- keep language/login/signup controls accessible

Do not simply shrink everything proportionally without reconsidering density.

---

## 12. Acceptance Criteria

The page is successful when all of the following are true:

1. The page clearly feels like a consumer media experience, not a dashboard.
2. The hero is obviously the focal point.
3. The play button feels polished and premium.
4. The topic field feels abundant but structured.
5. The left and right topic distribution feels balanced.
6. Near-center topic pills are short and controlled.
7. The top promo banner feels integrated and intentional.
8. The language selector, login, and sign up controls feel lightweight and premium.
9. Nothing overlaps.
10. The page feels bright, playful, and modern.

---

## 13. Implementation Priorities

If tradeoffs must be made, prioritize in this order:

1. Protected center hero readability
2. Correct play button styling
3. Balanced topic placement
4. Clean top banner execution
5. Decorative density

If a choice ever makes the page feel more dashboard-like, reject that choice.
If a choice makes the page feel cleaner, brighter, and more consumer-friendly, prefer that choice.
